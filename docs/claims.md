# Claims

- Mechanism claim tested: the smallest human correction that changes outcome should identify a local physical decision boundary more efficiently than full demonstrations or large residual corrections.
- Evidence claim: the v4 local benchmark tests task success, intervention magnitude, correction efficiency, damage, intent preservation, boundary error, ablations, and stress sweeps.
- Negative result: the learner improves efficiency and damage, but task-success gain over uncertainty querying is non-decisive and the full objective is not uniquely validated by success.
- Scope claim: results support archive-quality negative evidence, not real-robot deployment.
- Unsupported claim explicitly avoided: no claim of SOTA robot performance or ICLR-main readiness.
